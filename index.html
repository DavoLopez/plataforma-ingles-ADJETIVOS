Formación de Adjetivos

En inglés de nivel B2, no existe una regla única para la creación de adjetivos. Podemos formarlos a partir de sustantivos o verbos añadiendo sufijos al final de la palabra. Además, a menudo se utilizan prefijos negativos para invertir su significado.

Para otorgar a un sustantivo un carácter descriptivo, empleamos sufijos específicos:

  • -al, -ial (relacionado con): norm → normal, music → musical.
  • -ic, -ical (naturaleza de / causado por): romance → romantic.
  • -ful (lleno de / que tiene la cualidad): beauty → beautiful, harm → harmful.
  • -less (sin / que carece de): end → endless, speech → speechless.
  • -ish (origen / cualidad atenuada): child → childish, Britain → British.
  • -eous, -ious, -ous (cualidad / naturaleza): adventure → adventurous, ambition → ambitious.
  • -y (estado / aspecto superficial): anger → angry, wealth → wealthy.
  • -like / -ary (semejanza o relación): child → childlike, custom → customary.

La transformación de verbos a adjetivos describe la capacidad de realizar, sufrir o provocar una acción:

  • -ive (causa de un effecto activo): create → creative, talk → talkative, compete → competitive.
  • -able, -ible (viabilidad / posibilidad / aptitud): read → readable, break → breakable, rely → reliable.
  • -ful (plenitud): play → playful.
  • -ent, -ant (agente que ejecuta la acción): obey → obedient, expect → expectant.

Advertencia: El significado de estos adjetivos varía completamente dependiendo de su sufijo. Memoriza estas diferencias para evitar fallos típicos en exámenes.

Classic vs Classical

Classic: Excelente calidad, tradicional, que no pasa de moda.
"She was wearing a classic dark blue skirt."

Classical: Referente a la antigüedad grecorromana o artes formales académicas.
"Mozart is a classical composer."

Economic vs Economical

Economic: Relacionado con la economía global, finanzas o comercio del país.
"They discussed economic policy."

Economical: Ahorrativo, de bajo consumo de combustible o dinero.
"Hybrid cars are very economical."

Historic vs Historical

Historic: Hito de gran importancia o trascendencia en la historia.
"This is a historic moment for us."

Historical: Vinculado al estudio o documentos objetivos del pasado.
"I love reading historical novels."

Formal vs Informal Comparison (Anotación de Clase)

Informal Register (Common)

Utiliza un pronombre de objeto después de than:

1. "He is much taller than me."
2. "She runs faster than him."
3. "They are richer than us."
Formal Register (Academic)

Utiliza un pronombre de sujeto + verbo auxiliar después de than:

1. "He is much taller than I am."
2. "She runs faster than he does."
3. "They are richer than we are."

Direct Comparison with "Than"

Utilizamos "than" para conectar los dos elementos objeto de comparación directa:

1. "Madrid is much larger than Toledo."
2. "This exam was definitely more difficult than the last mock test."
3. "My new hybrid car runs far faster than my old diesel vehicle."
4. "This laptop is much lighter than my previous one."
5. "Our new office is significantly quieter than the old building."

Negative Comparison with "Less than"

Utilizamos "less" + adjetivo + "than" para indicar inferioridad:

1. "This smartphone is less expensive than the pro model."
2. "The secondary classroom is less noisy than the cafeteria."
3. "Our second group project was less successful than we had originally hoped."
4. "The offline version is less complicated than the online one."
5. "Her second presentation was less stressful than the first."

Equative Comparison: "As... as" & Negatives

Affirmative (Identical)
1. "This film is as interesting as the book."
2. "He is as tall as his older brother now."
3. "Your room is as clean as mine."
4. "This smartphone is as fast as a laptop."
5. "My score is as high as the class average."
Negative (Difference)
1. "The train is not as fast as the plane."
2. "The city centre is not as quiet as the suburbs."
3. "Today is not so warm as yesterday."
4. "Our second attempt was not as successful as the first."
5. "The modern apartment is not so spacious as the traditional villa."

Showing Differences or No Differences

🟢 Showing Small Differences

Adverbios y expresiones para detallar variaciones de grado muy pequeñas:

slightly: "The new printer is slightly faster than the old model."
a little: "This box is a little heavier than that one."
a bit: "Can you speak a bit louder, please?"
a little bit: "The bus was a little bit later than usual today."
not much: "The price is not much different from last year's."
similar to: "The local dialect is very similar to standard English."

⚪ Showing No Differences or Almost No Differences

Expresiones utilizadas para declarar que dos personas, lugares o cosas son completamente idénticas o casi idénticas:

almost: "My score is almost as high as yours."
nearly: "The second attempt was nearly as successful as the first."
not quite: "The temperature today is not quite as hot as yesterday."
roughly: "The two apartments are roughly the same size."
more or less: "The distance to the beach is more or less the same."
as ... as: "This cheap replica is as good as the original."
the same as: "Her dress design is exactly the same as mine."

💡 Intensifying "Similar to" with Very or Really

Podemos usar los intensificadores "very" o "really" antes de la expresión "similar to" para enfatizar una gran semejanza:

1. "The twin sisters are very similar to each other in personality."
2. "His painting style is really similar to Picasso's early artwork."
3. "This translation exercise is very similar to the one we did in class."

🔴 Describing Big Differences

Modificadores potentes para acentuar contrastes notables (cinco ejemplos por cada estructura):

much

- "This car is much faster than the previous model."

- "Learning English is much easier with a native tutor."

- "The food here is much better than I expected."

- "This route is much safer during the winter."

- "The movie was much longer than the book."

so much

- "The office atmosphere is so much better today."

- "This book was so much more thrilling."

- "He feels so much happier in his new school."

- "The second exam was so much easier than the first one."

- "I have so much more energy after a good sleep."

a lot

- "He feels a lot healthier since he changed his diet."

- "Living in a village is a lot cheaper than London."

- "This laptop is a lot lighter than my old one."

- "The new software is a lot more reliable."

- "We are a lot more prepared for the B2 test now."

quite

- "The syllabus of level B2 is quite different."

- "This task is quite a lot harder than yesterday's."

- "The results are quite different from our expectations."

- "The weather is quite a bit warmer today than yesterday."

- "This exercise is quite a deal more complex."

even

- "The exam was hard, but the retake was even harder."

- "It's cold now, but tomorrow will be even colder."

- "He speaks fast, but his brother talks even faster."

- "The first project was good, but this one is even more impressive."

- "Trains are fast, but planes are even quicker."

different from

- "My current school is very different from my old one."

- "This system of grading is highly different from ours."

- "His opinion is completely different from his father's."

- "The new design is totally different from the draft."

- "Life in the UK is really different from life in Spain."

far

- "The results are far superior to what we projected."

- "It is far more expensive to book flights in summer."

- "She is far more creative than her peers."

- "The destination is far wetter than we anticipated."

- "His new explanation was far easier to understand."

⚡ Emphasizing with "Nowhere near as ... as"

Se utiliza para denotar de forma rotunda que algo no es ni de lejos parecido o de igual calidad a otro elemento (5 ejemplos):

1. "The local cinema is nowhere near as big as the IMAX theater."
2. "The grammar test was nowhere near as easy as I expected."
3. "My second draft was nowhere near as good as the first one."
4. "The local team is nowhere near as skilled as the national champions."
5. "This copy is nowhere near as clear as the original document."

💥 Adverbs with "Different From"

Usamos intensificadores absolutos (very, really, completely, totally) para marcar una divergencia completa (4 ejemplos):

1. "The final score was completely different from what the experts predicted."
2. "My sister's artistic lifestyle is totally different from mine."
3. "The weather in Scotland is really different from the Spanish climate."
4. "This new textbook is very different from the one we used last semester."

📈 Repeated Comparatives for Progressive Change

Utilizamos adjetivos comparativos repetidos unidos por "and" para describir un cambio progresivo y continuo (5 ejemplos):

1. "The exams are getting tougher and tougher as we approach level C1."
2. "Life is becoming more and more expensive due to high inflation rates."
3. "The winter weather is getting colder and colder each week."
4. "The days are getting shorter and shorter as winter approaches."
5. "The software is becoming more and more complex with every update."

Superlativos

We use superlative adjectives to compare one person, thing, place or situation to the whole group or category it belongs to.

(Usamos adjetivos superlativos para comparar una persona, cosa, lugar o situación con todo el grupo o categoría al que pertenece).

📊 Spelling Rules for Superlatives

Adjective Type Rule 5 Examples
Monosílabos
(Most one syllable adjectives)		 + -est
tallest coolest strongest cheapest highest
CVC
(One syllable ending in CVC)		 Double consonant + -est

Acabados en Y
(Two-syllables ending in -y)		 Change -y to -i + -est
happiest driest easiest busiest heaviest
Polisílabos
(Two syllables or more)		 most + adjective
most expensive most beautiful most dangerous most interesting most creative

Use of "the" with Superlatives

Por lo general colocamos "the" antes del adjetivo superlativo para delimitar al único elemento superior en la categoría (5 ejemplos):

1. "She is the tallest girl in our classroom."
2. "This is the most expensive restaurant in town."
3. "Monday is usually the busiest day of the week."
4. "He bought the most powerful computer available."
5. "That was the worst film I have ever seen."

Preposition "in" for Places and Groups

Después de un superlativo, solemos utilizar "in" antes de referirnos a un lugar singular o a un grupo u organización en vez de "of" (5 ejemplos):

1. "He is the smartest student in the school."
2. "This is the oldest castle in Scotland."
3. "She is the most experienced designer in our team."
4. "Mount Everest is the highest mountain in the world."
5. "That was the most dramatic moment in the history of the club."

Emphasis with "By far" + Superlative

Usamos la expresión "by far" justo antes del superlativo para enfatizar una diferencia enorme en un grupo (5 ejemplos):

1. "This is by far the most interesting book in the library."
2. "He is by far the best player in the team."
3. "That was by far the worst decision in our company's history."
4. "Tokyo is by far the largest city in Japan."
5. "This hybrid car is by far the most economical vehicle on the market."

Ejercicio 4: Readings (Formación)

Lee los siguientes dos textos de nivel B2 y responde las preguntas de selección múltiple correspondientes.

Text 1: The Unexpected Eco-Adventure

Last summer, my sister and I decided to go on a highly adventurous journey to the Scottish Highlands. We had always dreamed of visiting the historic castles that are famous worldwide. Since petrol is extremely expensive these days, we rented a hybrid car, which turned out to be incredibly economical and perfect for our budget.

However, the weather on the first day was absolutely miserable due to heavy rain. We almost made a careless mistake by taking a mountain trail without a GPS. It was a tricky road, and with our fuel running low, arguing was completely pointless.

Fortunately, we found a local B&B owned by a wonderfully cheerful couple. They served us a warm, meaty stew that saved our evening. We were immensely relieved to be safe. It was an experience that proved how being well-prepared and making sensible decisions is key when exploring remote places.

Text 2: The Historical Restoration Project

The local council decided to fund a historical restoration project in the city center. The building in question was a classical concert hall, once known as the finest example of Victorian architecture. For years, the hall was left in a highly neglected state, looking completely useless and messy.

Some critics argued that spending money on this was a foolish idea during times of general economic crisis. They claimed that the budget could have been used for more useful public services. However, the conservationists were very hopeful that the hall would once again attract thousands of tourists.

The outcome has been absolutely impressive. After months of hard work, the concert hall reopened last night. The acoustic upgrades are magnificent, and the opening show was a classic success. Everyone now agrees that keeping our heritage alive is deeply logical and rewarding.

Ejercicio 5: Práctica de Comparativos

Reading 1: The Tech Upgrade Dilemma (Symmetrical Structure)

Choosing a new smartphone is becoming more and more difficult because of constant innovations. Today, we compare the Alpha X and the Beta Prime.

On the one hand, the Alpha X is slightly faster than the Beta Prime, making it great for multitaskers, though it is also a good deal larger, which some users might find uncomfortable. On the other hand, the Beta Prime is not quite as heavy as the Alpha X. Its battery life is significantly better when running demanding apps.

In terms of cost, the Beta Prime is nowhere near as expensive as the Alpha X, which represents a massive difference for budget-conscious buyers. In conclusion, although the Alpha X is a bit more advanced, the Beta Prime is much more practical for everyday use.

Reading 2: City Life vs Country Life

Deciding where to settle down is far more complex than it seems.

On the one hand, living in the city is much more exciting because you have endless entertainment options, and public transport is significantly more efficient. However, the air is a bit more polluted and rent is a good deal more expensive.

On the other hand, countryside living is nowhere near as stressful as city life. The landscape is infinitely more beautiful, and people are usually much friendlier. Although finding a high-paying job is slightly harder in a small village, the cost of living is a lot cheaper, making it an attractive option for families.

Reading 3: Two Career Paths: Corporate vs Freelance

Graduates often face a tough decision when starting their professional careers.

On the one hand, a corporate job offers far greater stability and a significantly more predictable monthly salary, which makes financial planning a lot easier. However, the corporate work environment can be much more rigid and competitive.

On the other hand, freelancing is nowhere near as secure in the beginning, but it gives you much greater freedom over your daily schedule. Managing your own time is infinitely more rewarding, even though the administrative duties are a good deal more demanding than those in a standard office position.

Ejercicio 6: Práctica de Superlativos

Reading 1: The Architecture Awards

Last week, the city council hosted the most prestigious architectural awards in the country. The jury looked for designs that represented the pinnacle of sustainable innovation.

The winner of the main prize was the Eco-Tower. It is by far the tallest green building in Europe, standing at three hundred meters. The engineers installed the most powerful solar system available, which generates enough energy to power the entire neighborhood.

In contrast, the runner-up, a timber library, was noted for having the quietest study rooms in the world. It was by far the most popular design among the public vote. The head of the committee remarked that this year presented the toughest decision in the history of the awards.

Reading 2: The Extreme Explorers

Last winter, a team of daring scientists set out on the most dangerous expedition in polar history.

Their mission was to reach the coldest and the most remote weather station in Antarctica. The team wore the most advanced thermal gear available to survive the freezing winds. The expedition leader remarked that this was by far the toughest physical challenge of their lives.

Despite facing the worst blizzard of the season, they successfully gathered crucial data, making this journey the most successful scientific endeavor of the year.

Reading 3: The Grand Culinary Championship

Yesterday, the city hosted the most exciting culinary competition of the year.

Chefs from the finest restaurants gathered to win the Golden Spoon award. Each participant had to create a dish using the most unusual local ingredients. The judges agreed that deciding on a winner was the hardest task they had ever faced.

In the end, the first prize went to a young chef who prepared a dessert with by far the most creative presentation. It was hailed as the most impressive culinary masterpiece of the decade.
